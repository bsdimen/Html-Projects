Timeline of Notable Monuments in Andalusia

785

The Mezquita

Construction Began: 785

Completed: 987

The beauty of Córdoba’s great mosque cannot be overstated. Its vast, serene interior remains peaceful even amid the crowds. As one of the finest examples of Islamic architecture, the Mezquita reflects a period of cultural richness when Muslims, Jews, and Christians coexisted and thrived together, enriching the city with their vibrant, diverse cultures. Arab chronicles tell how Abd ar-Rahman I initially bought half of the Visigothic church of San Vicente for Friday prayers and, in AD 784, acquired the rest to build a new mosque. Three later expansions nearly quintupled its size, resulting in the mosque we see today—with one notable change: a Christian cathedral placed right in its center during the 16th century, giving rise to its name ‘Mezquita-Catedral’.
