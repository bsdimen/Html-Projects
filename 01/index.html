Unveil the Treasures of Andalusia

Explore iconic monuments such as the Alhambra, Mezquita of Córdoba, and the Alcázar of Seville.

The Alhambra

The Alhambra

A stunning palace and fortress complex located in Granada. The Alhambra is renowned for its intricate Islamic art, lush gardens, and breathtaking views of the Sierra Nevada mountains. Explore its grand halls, including the Nasrid Palaces and the enchanting Generalife gardens.

The Alhambra

The Mezquita (Mosque-Cathedral of Córdoba)

This magnificent structure in Córdoba showcases the harmonious blend of Islamic and Christian architecture. Originally built as a mosque in the 8th century, it features stunning horseshoe arches, intricate mosaics, and an impressive Renaissance cathedral within its walls.

The Alcázar of Seville

The Alcázar of Seville

A royal palace originally developed by Moorish Muslim kings, the Alcázar is famous for its stunning Mudejar architecture, lush gardens, and elaborate tile work. It's one of the oldest palaces still in use in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

The Giralda Towe

The Giralda Towe

Adjacent to Seville Cathedral, this former minaret was constructed during the Islamic period and is now a bell tower. Climb its ramp to enjoy panoramic views of Seville, and marvel at its beautiful Renaissance architecture.

The Roman Theatre of Mérida

The Roman Theatre of Mérida

This magnificent structure in Córdoba showcases the harmonious blend of Islamic and Christian architecture. Originally built as a mosque in the 8th century, it features stunning horseshoe arches, intricate mosaics, and an impressive Renaissance cathedral within its walls.

The Alcazaba of Almería

The Alcazaba of Almería

This impressive fortress in Almería was built in the 10th century and offers stunning views of the city and the Mediterranean Sea. Explore its well-preserved walls, towers, and gardens.

The Palacio de los Reyes Católicos

The Palacio de los Reyes Católicos (Palace of the Catholic Monarchs)

Located in Málaga, this palace was built in the 15th century and served as a residence for the Catholic Monarchs. Its stunning architecture and historical significance make it a must-visit.

The Santa Maria la Blanca

The Santa Maria la Blanca

Once a synagogue and later converted into a church, this stunning building in Seville is known for its Mudejar architecture, featuring whitewashed walls and beautiful arches.

The Patio de los Naranjos

The Patio de los Naranjos

Located next to the Cathedral of Seville, this former mosque courtyard is adorned with orange trees and fountains, offering a serene escape in the heart of the city.

The Castillo de Gibralfaro

The Castillo de Gibralfaro

Overlooking the city of Málaga, this ancient Moorish castle provides breathtaking views of the coastline and the city below. Discover its fascinating history and explore the well-preserved ruins.
